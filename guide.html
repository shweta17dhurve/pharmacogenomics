User Guide

This user guide explains how to navigate and use the Pharmacogenomics Portal effectively. The portal integrates pharmacogenomic information related to cardiovascular drugs and allows users to explore gene–drug interactions, visualize biological relationships, and understand how genetic variation may influence drug response.

Purpose of the Portal

The Pharmacogenomics Portal was developed to demonstrate how genetic information can be integrated with drug data in a web-based database. The system allows users to explore the relationship between genes and cardiovascular drugs and understand how these interactions can affect drug metabolism and therapeutic outcomes.

The portal serves as an educational and research tool for students, researchers, and healthcare professionals interested in pharmacogenomics and personalized medicine.

How to Use the Search System

The search system allows users to retrieve pharmacogenomic information about specific drugs or genes.

  • Go to the Home Page of the portal.
  • Enter the name of a drug or gene in the search bar.
  • Click the Search button.
  • The system will retrieve pharmacogenomic information related to the searched drug.
  • A detailed drug profile page will be displayed.

Understanding the Drug Profile Page

When a drug is searched, the portal displays a detailed pharmacogenomic profile containing multiple sections of information.

  • Drug Overview: Provides a brief description of the drug and its therapeutic application.
  • Gene Information: Displays the gene associated with the drug metabolism or response.
  • Clinical Significance: Explains the clinical importance of the gene–drug interaction.
  • Mechanism of Action: Describes how the drug works at the molecular or biochemical level.
  • Drug Response: Indicates how genetic variation may affect the effectiveness of the drug.
  • Scientific References: Provides references to research publications related to the drug.

Understanding the Gene–Drug Visualization

The portal includes graphical visualization tools to help users understand the relationships between genes and drugs.

The bar graph visualizes the interaction between genes and drugs present in the database. Each bar represents a gene associated with the selected drug. Visualization helps users quickly identify which genes are involved in drug metabolism or therapeutic response.

Understanding the Gene–Drug Interaction Network

The gene–drug interaction network provides a graphical representation of relationships between pharmacogenomic genes and drugs.

  • Nodes represent genes or drugs.
  • Edges represent interactions between genes and drugs.
  • The network visualization helps users identify biological connections between genetic factors and pharmacological compounds.

Such network-based visualization is commonly used in bioinformatics to interpret complex biological relationships.

Using the Dose Recommendation Calculator

The portal includes a dose recommendation calculator that demonstrates how pharmacogenomic information may influence drug dosing decisions.

To use the calculator:

  • Select the metabolizer genotype category.
  • Enter the patient age.
  • Enter the patient body weight.
  • Click the Calculate Dose button.

The system will generate a simulated dose recommendation based on the entered information. This feature demonstrates how genetic variation and patient characteristics may influence drug dosing.

Exploring the Database Sections

The portal provides several database exploration tools that allow users to browse available pharmacogenomic information.

  • Gene Explorer: Displays genes involved in pharmacogenomic drug metabolism.
  • Drug Explorer: Shows the list of drugs available in the database.
  • Dashboard: Provides summary statistics about the database including the total number of genes, drugs, and interactions.

External Resource Links

The portal also provides links to external biomedical databases where users can explore additional scientific information about drugs and genes.

These resources contain research publications, genomic data, and pharmacological information related to the drug being studied.

Data Interpretation Notes

The information presented in this portal is intended to illustrate the principles of pharmacogenomics and gene–drug interactions. Users should interpret the results as educational or research insights into how genetic variation can influence drug response.

Limitations of the Portal

This portal is designed for educational and research demonstration purposes. While the system presents pharmacogenomic concepts and relationships, clinical decisions should always be made using validated medical guidelines and professional medical consultation.

Example Use Case

A researcher searches for a cardiovascular drug in the portal. The system displays the pharmacogenomic profile of the drug, including the associated gene, mechanism of action, clinical significance, visualization graphs, and gene–drug interaction network.

The researcher can then explore how genetic variation might influence drug metabolism and therapeutic response, providing insights into personalized medicine approaches.
