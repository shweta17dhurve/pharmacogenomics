Home

This pharmacogenomic portal is designed to support the exploration of gene–drug interactions involved in cardiovascular therapy, especially myocardial infarction and related disorders. It helps users understand how genetic variation can influence drug metabolism, response, safety, and therapeutic effectiveness.

The platform integrates pharmacogenomic concepts with a searchable web interface so that students, researchers, and healthcare professionals can review drug information, target genes, clinical significance, interaction patterns, and visual representations of gene–drug networks.

About

Pharmacogenomics is the study of how genetic variation affects drug response. In cardiovascular medicine, genes such as CYP2C19, CYP2C9, and VKORC1 can influence the effectiveness and safety of drugs like clopidogrel and warfarin. A dedicated pharmacogenomic portal helps organize this information in a structured and searchable form.

This website is intended to serve as an educational and research tool, demonstrating how bioinformatics can be used to integrate, visualize, and interpret pharmacogenomic data for personalized medicine.

User Guide

1. Search for a drug or gene using the search section above.
2. Open the detailed drug page for line-wise pharmacogenomic information.
3. Explore graphs and gene–drug interaction networks to interpret relationships visually.
4. Use the dose calculator on the drug page to simulate genotype-based dosing suggestions.
5. Browse the database using the Explore the Database section.
